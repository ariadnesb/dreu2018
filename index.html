My Projects

During my time in Minnesota so far I have worked on two projects. One of the projects is developing a 2D video game interface for a robot designed to help stroke patients regain control of their wrist movements. The robot was designed in Dr. Jurgen's lab here at the University of Minnesota. The robotic arm helps patients who have suffered a stroke them with the exercises they need to do to for regaining motion and sensory control over their arm. However, these exercises are extremely repetitive and boring. Many patients never complete them because of this. One of the projects that I will be working on this summer is designing a game that will work with the robot to try and make it more enjoyable for the people using the device so that they complete their exercises and are able to regain more function of their arm.

Another project that I will work on this summer will be using virtual reality to see if the size of the elements in wallpaper patterns effects how big people perceive a room to be. Virtual reality provides us with a way to immerse people in different environments without having to physically build these environments. It also allows us to control for many other factors such as lighting, room size, ceiling height, ect. We will see if the scale of the wallpaper texture effect both people's subjective judgement of the spaciousness of a room and their action based egocentric judgements of distance in a room.
